Mahatma Gandhi

Father of Nation

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist, who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule, and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific, first applied to him in 1914 in South Africa, is now used throughout the world.

All movements by Mahatma gandhi:

  • Champaran agitations
  • Kheda agitations
  • Khilafat movement
  • Non-co-operation
  • Salt Satyagraha (Salt March)
  • World War II and Quit India movement
